For the more complex task of extracting data with specific column or index labels, we can use .loc. The .loc accessor allows
us to specify the labels of rows and columns we wish to extract. The labels (commonly referred to as the indices) are the bold
text on the far left of a DataFrame, while the column labels are the column names found at the top of a DataFrame.
To grab data with .loc, we must specify the row and column label(s) where the data exists. The row labels are the first
argument to the .loc function; the column labels are the second.
Arguments to .loc can be:
A single value.
A slice.
A list.
